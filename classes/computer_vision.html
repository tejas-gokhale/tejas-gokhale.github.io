Office Hours

Office hours are your time to ask questions and get clarification on concepts from the course. We encourage going to TA office hours for questions about homeworks, projects, implementation, etc. and faculty office hours for conceptual questions.

Pre-requisites

We will assume that you have a basic (but solid) expertise in linear algebra, geometry, probability, and Python programming. Recommended classes at UMBC are: MATH 221 (Linear Algebra), STAT 355 or CMPE 320 (Probability and Statistics), MATH 151 (Calculus and Analytical Geometry). If you are unfamiliar with linear algebra or calculus, you should consider taking both: without these tools, you are likely to struggle with the course. Although we will provide brief math refreshers of these necessary topics, CMSC 491/691 should not be your first introduction to these topics. Homework 0 (an ungraded homework that will NOT contribute to your grade) is meant to be a reflection of these prerequisites. If you struggle with HW0, please get in touch with the instructor as soon as possible to discuss remedial options before the drop deadline (09/13).
We understand that some students may have had some prior exposure to signal/image/audio processing, computer graphics, machine learning, etc. However, none of these are pre-requisites -- the class is designed to be self-contained.

Reference Books

There are no required textbooks. The following books may be useful to accompany the lectures:

  • Computer Vision: Algorithms and Applications by Szeliski. Available for free online.
  • Deep Learning by Goodfellow, Bengio, and Courville. Available for free online. See Part I for brushing up on pre-requisities mentioned above.

Projects

The class has a mix of PhD, MS, and BS students. Projects will be judged on the basis of relative growth (from where you start to where you end). This means that Type-I projects and Type-II projects will have different goals and evaluation criteria.

  • Type-I: PhD or MS (thesis) students Come see me and discuss your existing research agenda. We will integrate the course project into that agenda if possible to avoid double work. I recommend working in groups of 1-3 students.
  • Type-II: BS or MS (coursework) students Feel free to pick one of the suggested topics. If you want to work on a cool idea of your own, come see me and we can create a concrete structure and gameplan. I recommend working in groups of 3-4 students.
